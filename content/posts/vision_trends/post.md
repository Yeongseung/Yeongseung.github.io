# Introduction

Recent developments in autonomous robots are moving toward integrating LLM's knowledge, using VLM (Visual Language Model) and VLA (Visual Language Action model). 

In [a recent NVIDIA post](https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/building-generalist-humanoid-capabilities-with-nvidia-isaac-gr00t-n1-6-using-a-sim-to-real-workflow/), which demonstrates remarkable work in robotics, VLM and VLA play key roles. The idea itself is quite simple: VLM performs high-level reasoning, and VLA takes the reasoning and outputs actions (though it's just part of a larger architecture).

However, how can VLM and VLA be trained, and what do they look like? I'd like to explore recent vision model advances that have led to VLM and VLA.
